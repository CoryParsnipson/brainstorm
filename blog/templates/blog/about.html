Slack Boss

One more thing: don’t mistake Slacker Zen for the popular definition of a slacker—someone who works at a dead end job contributing nothing to society, or someone who dodges military service. A slacker, in the proper sense of the word, is someone who is true to themselves and seeks to do what is best (read: most interesting) at the moment.

For example, Mark Zuckerberg, Steve Jobs, and Bill Gates are among the ranks of famous modern-day slackers. They dropped out of college halfway through and accidentally made huge companies. Amazing what kinds of things you can do by accident? Anyway, not all slackers are huge computer nerds. Gandhi was another famous slacker who was able to bring about widespread social change over his refusal to do anything at all. It was quite amazing, watching him sit there doing nothing, being so in tune with his Slacker Zen that he could not even muster the will to eat food until he almost died. Similarly, Rosa Parks, did the same almost fifty years later, being such a Zen master, that she was unable to get up from her seat on the bus. This singular non-action would have epic consequences reverberating to the present day. And most of all, don’t forget me, Cory Parsnipson. At the ripe old age of 23, I am already a professional slacker with an impressive resume of nothing-doings already under my belt.

If you are reading this and you still think anyone who calls themselves a slacker is an unambitious piece of shit, then you are completely misunderstanding. Just go back to your cubicle and press those buttons and prepare those spreadsheets that are going to be thrown away the instant you finish them and do that forever. This kind of stuff is probably not for you. Sorry. We could have had cake together. :(

Just take it easy. Be a Slacker. Live your life.
